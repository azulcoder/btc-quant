btc·quant

An honest Bitcoin quant research & backtest terminal — live public data, client-side backtests, a real option chain, and live charts. Built to see through a flattering backtest, not to sell one.

Open the live terminal → View source on GitHub

What it does

  • Honesty-first backtests — every result is net of cost, with out-of-sample / walk-forward, a Deflated Sharpe (Bailey & López de Prado), and buy-and-hold as the baseline. The headline is the deflated Sharpe, never a pretty curve.
  • Strategy leaderboard — every strategy ranked vs buy-and-hold; most don't beat it after costs + deflation, and the terminal shows you that plainly.
  • Live option chain (Deribit) — IV smile/skew, ATM term structure vs DVOL, 25Δ risk-reversal, and a VRP / short-vol view framed by CVaR & max-drawdown, not Sharpe.
  • Live charts — TradingView Advanced Chart + exchange WebSocket ticking, kept visually separate from the discrete backtest bars.
  • Python engine — pandas/numpy/scipy; daily & hourly; a compare leaderboard CLI and a matplotlib tearsheet.

The honest stance

  • No one can predict markets; most retail edges decay. A backtested Sharpe > 2 in crypto is a red flag; > 3 is almost certainly an artifact.
  • The durable edge here is risk management + the variance-risk-premium exposure, not directional alpha — and the tool is built to demonstrate that skepticism.
  • It places no orders and holds no API keys. Every data source is public. Nothing is fabricated — on failure a panel says so.
