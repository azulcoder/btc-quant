    VRP / short-vol risk-premium exposure — implied (Deribit DVOL) vs realized vol SIGNAL · risk-premium stale

    —

    —

    DVOL is Deribit's BTC 30-day implied-vol index; realized is the trailing 30-bar annualized vol from this terminal's own returns. VRP = implied − realized, large and persistently positive (IV > RV ~70% of the time) — the price paid for crash/vol-spike protection (Carr-Wu 2009; Almeida et al. 2025: BTC VRP ≈ 7× equity). Harvest it by being short vol, but the payoff is sharply negatively skewed and tail-lethal: Deribit's cost-inclusive weekend-strangle backtest made +29.7% APR yet drew down 23.8% (≈45% on daily). Report it with CVaR / max-DD, never headline Sharpe — and it is NOT a sell button (the BTC VRP→return relation is negative, the opposite of equities; brief §2.1). Degrades gracefully if Deribit is unreachable.

    Option IV smile / skew — OTM-only, per expiry DESCRIPTIVE · sentiment stale

    —

    A MARK smile from mark_iv only (the one public get_book_summary_by_currency call has no per-contract bid/ask IV), gated to the OTM side (puts below F, calls above F), both bid&ask present, and |BS-delta| ≥ 0.05; interpolated shape-preserving (PCHIP, linear fallback) — never a wiggly cubic spline that manufactures butterfly arbitrage (brief §1.4a, §1.7).     ATM IV term structure + DVOL overlay SIGNAL · vol-forecast / regime stale

    —

    ATMF IV (interpolated at F) vs days-to-expiry across the live chain, ACT/365 to 08:00 UTC (brief §1.5). The existing DVOL line is overlaid as the 30-day model-free benchmark: DVOL sits ABOVE pure ATM IV by roughly the convexity (BF) premium — the gap is the smile's contribution, not an error (brief §1.6). Cross-tenor interpolation, where done, must be in total variance (w = IV²·T), never in IV, or you manufacture calendar arbitrage. Use the slope (e.g. front vs ~90d) for regime & vol-sizing, never as a buy/sell (brief §2.2).

    25-delta risk reversal (RR25) & butterfly (BF25) DESCRIPTIVE · sentiment stale

    ATM IV (front)—
    RR25 (front)—25dC − 25dP
    BF25 (front)—½(C+P) − ATM

    —

    Pinned sign convention (brief §1.4d-e): RR25 = IV(25Δ call) − IV(25Δ put) (call-minus-put). RR25 < 0 ⇒ the 25Δ put is richer ⇒ downside protection bid up (the typical BTC "put skew" / fear read); RR25 > 0 ⇒ call skew (euphoria). BF25 = ½(IV25c+IV25p) − IVATM is smile convexity. The ±25Δ strikes are solved on a plain Black-Scholes spot delta on the observed IV (F as forward, r=0) — a Deribit-style 25Δ, NOT the FX premium-adjusted 25Δ (brief §1.4d caveat). The lower chart is RR25(T), the term-of-skew (front-end noisier/steeper). Sentiment gauge, not a return-timing signal (brief §2.3).

    On-chain context — network activity (descriptive, NOT a signal) stale

    —

    Context only — explicitly NOT a tradeable signal. On-chain / sentiment series are mostly descriptive, not predictive, and carry a look-ahead / revision trap larger than trading cost: values for past dates get retroactively revised, so a backtest on today's series is lying about what was knowable then (RESEARCH.md 2.17). Shown as background context, never deflated/backtested as alpha. Source: blockchain.info charts API; degrades gracefully on failure.

    Engine mirror in vanilla JS: quant.js reimplements features / backtest / risk from the Python btcquant package (shift-by-1, turnover cost, deflated Sharpe). Charts are dependency-free SVG (charts.js). Data is live and public (Coinbase / Kraken / CoinGecko / Bybit); on failure the terminal shows a stale-data banner rather than fabricating anything.

    Edges decay; treat every signal as a hypothesis to re-test. A backtested Sharpe > 2 in crypto is a red flag; > 3 is almost certainly an artifact. See DISCLAIMER.md.
